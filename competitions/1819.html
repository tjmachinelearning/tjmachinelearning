This year, in collaboration with professors at John Hopkins, we will hold a series of long-term competitions for club members to participate. Based on the rankings for these competitions, we will select interested members for machine learning internships with these professors during the coming summer. Because this competition ends very early on in the year, when we select the final members for the internship, we will take into account that new members have not had the sufficient experience to perform well in this competition when considering the rankings. New members are welcome to participate, but this competition is primarily for advanced, returning members. Please see the individual competition instructions below.

RSNA Pneumonia Competition Instructions

Competition Introduction

Our advanced members can compete in the RSNA Pneumonia competition. Detailed rules and instructions can be found on the Kaggle competition page. In short, the goal is to predict whether a patient has pneumonia or not based on images of patients' chest radiographs and if the image indicates the patient having pneumonia to predict where the lung opacities (visual signals that the patient has pneumonia) on the chest radiograph are.

There are two stages to the competition.

Stage 1: Until 10/5

Members submit models individually

Stage 2: Until 10/15

Top 4 members merge into 1 team and create a collective model to the competition.

Members may start creating/submitting models now. More detailed instructions to come. Competition specifics and strategies will be covered in the meeting on 9/26, but it is recommended that you get started beforehand.
